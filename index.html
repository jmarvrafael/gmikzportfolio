Artist Statement

Kim Gómez-Zelaya (pronounced cel-AH-ya) boasting my elongated Latinx surname is always a pleasure. Born in Queens, NY it’s been a privilege being surrounded by works of art giving me an affinity to experience, understand, and become infatuated with it. My work exemplifies an utterly deranged yet managed situation; the effect given is honest and outspoken. Photography being my home base, the discovery of infusing various creative mediums has evolved the idea of a “simple image” into a construct of large assemblages. The use of vibrant colors distinctive to my Latinx American roots, combining it with a splash of muted tonalities, make each piece arise due to its apparent contrast. I presume true that a story is told by my subjects, they embody both the masculine and feminine essence; androgyny is conveyed into its ambiguous form. What I do is let myself go; my innermost chaos is visually displayed giving viewers an array of countless emotions.
